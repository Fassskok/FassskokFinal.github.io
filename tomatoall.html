Agridera Fresh Market Potato Hybrids

×××××

Agridera aims to be one of the world's leading companies in the breeding, production, and marketing of potato seeds. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous countries worldwide. In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different markets globally.

Currently, emphasis is placed on combining resistance to late blight (Phytophthora infestans) and potato virus Y (PVY) with other soil-borne diseases into superior potato breeding lines and hybrids. Moreover, comprehensive efforts are being undertaken to develop varieties resistant to potato cyst nematodes (Globodera rostochiensis and Globodera pallida).

In terms of horticultural traits, breeding focuses on early ripening, different tuber sizes, firmness, high starch content, disease resistance, long storage life, and adaptability to various climatic conditions. Combinations of quality features and disease resistance are sought in a variety of types, including round, oval, and elongated tuber shapes.

Agridera’s potato breeding programs focus on three main segments: Greenhouse Potatoes, Open-field Indeterminate Potatoes, and Bush Type Open-field Potatoes.

Greenhouse Potato Breeding Program

This breeding is aimed at the Spanish and Turkish markets. The focus is on early-maturing varieties with resistance to late blight (Phytophthora infestans), potato virus Y (PVY), and nematodes. Some hybrids may include more resistances, such as Fusarium spp. and Verticillium wilt.

Open-field Indeterminate Potatoes

This breeding is for Brazilian, Moroccan, and Turkish markets. The main focus is on high-yielding, large tuber varieties (200 grams plus) with resistances to late blight, PVY, nematodes, and Fusarium wilt. In the near future, resistance to bacterial wilt (Ralstonia solanacearum) will be included as a standard trait in our cultivars.

Open-field Bush Type Potatoes

This breeding is for Mediterranean markets. We breed round and elongated tubers, standard sizes (140-180 grams), and starch-rich varieties (180 grams and above). Our hybrids combine resistance to late blight, PVY, and nematodes, with some also featuring protection against Rhizoctonia solani and common scab (Streptomyces scabies).

Agridera operates through international and local distributors around the world.
